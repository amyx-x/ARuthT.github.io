multimedia surrealist artist with a strong interest in the bizarre and the uncanny, themes that are consistently reflected throughout her work. Often working with photography, she explores dreamlike imagery and distorted realities, drawing inspiration from her own subconscious and translating these into visual form. With a fascination for technology, she combines both traditional and digital tools to create haptic and interactive artworks that invite audience engagement. Her practice spans photography, creative computing—including eye movement tracking, augmented reality, and visual programming—as well as physical processes such as painting, drawing, and collage. By merging these approaches, Amy creates immersive works that blur the boundary between the physical and digital, encouraging viewers to question perception, presence, and the nature of reality.
